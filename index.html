🗼 About the Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is one of the most iconic and recognizable structures in the entire world. Standing tall in the heart of Paris, France, it was originally built as a temporary exhibition entrance arch for the 1889 World's Fair — the Exposition Universelle — which celebrated the 100th anniversary of the French Revolution. Today, it stands as the most visited paid monument on the planet.

Located on the Champ de Mars in the 7th arrondissement of Paris, the Eiffel Tower sits along the banks of the Seine River. It was designed and engineered by Gustave Eiffel, whose company specialized in metal frameworks and large-scale iron structures. The tower reaches a total height of 330 meters (1,083 feet) including its broadcast antenna, making it the tallest structure in Paris.

To build something on the scale of the Eiffel Tower is to leave a permanent mark on civilization — iron turned into poetry.

— Gustave Eiffel, Engineer & Architect

Constructed between 1887 and 1889, the tower was initially met with mixed reactions from Parisians. Many artists and intellectuals called it an eyesore and demanded its removal after the fair. However, its strategic value as a powerful radio transmission tower saved it from demolition once its original 20-year permit expired. Over the following decades, it transformed from a controversial lattice of iron into the beloved global symbol it is today.

The Eiffel Tower holds immense historical and cultural importance. It represented the cutting-edge industrial engineering of the 19th century and demonstrated what human creativity, mathematics, and iron could achieve together. It remains an enduring symbol of French ingenuity, romance, and artistic ambition — visited by nearly 7 million people every year, making it a cornerstone of global tourism and cultural heritage.

📅 Historical Timeline

1887
Construction begins on January 28th on the Champ de Mars, Paris.
1889
Tower is completed and inaugurated for the World's Fair on March 31st.
1909
Saved from demolition — repurposed as a military radio transmission tower.
1930
Loses title of world's tallest structure to New York's Chrysler Building.
1985
The iconic nighttime golden lighting system is installed.
Today
Welcomes over 7 million visitors annually — the world's most visited monument.

💡 Interesting Facts

  • Made of ~7,300 tonnes of wrought iron held together by 2.5 million rivets.
  • Was the world's tallest structure for 41 years until the Chrysler Building (1930).
  • Repainted every 7 years using around 60 tonnes of paint to prevent rust.
  • Can grow up to 15 cm taller in summer due to thermal expansion of iron.
  • Has 1,665 steps and three separate observation decks at different heights.
  • Sparkles with 20,000 bulbs every evening — five minutes each hour after sunset.
  • Gustave Eiffel had a private apartment at the very top of the tower.
  • About 300 workers spent over 2 years assembling the tower's 18,000 parts.
